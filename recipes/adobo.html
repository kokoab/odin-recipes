Adobo

Description

Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish known for its tangy, savory, and slightly sweet flavor profile. This braised dish, often made with chicken or pork, is cooked in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, and spices, resulting in tender meat with a rich, flavorful sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken thighs or pork belly, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed (optional)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, marinate chicken or pork in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Saut√© onion until translucent.
  3. Add marinated meat (reserve marinade) and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Pour in reserved marinade and water. Add brown sugar and stir.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Simmer for 30-40 minutes until meat is tender.
  6. If using potatoes, add them halfway through cooking and simmer until soft.
  7. Adjust seasoning if needed. Serve hot with steamed rice.
