Pansit

Description

Pansit, also spelled pancit, is a traditional Filipino noodle dish that’s a staple at gatherings and celebrations. This stir-fried dish combines rice noodles with a colorful mix of vegetables, meat, and a savory sauce, offering a balance of flavors and textures that’s both light and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz rice noodles (bihon)
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup green beans, sliced
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Steps

  1. Soak rice noodles in warm water for 10 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  3. Add chicken slices and cook until lightly browned, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add shrimp and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Stir in carrots, green beans, and cabbage. Cook for 3-4 minutes until vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, and chicken broth. Stir to combine.
  7. Add drained noodles and toss to coat evenly. Cook until noodles are soft and have absorbed the sauce, about 5 minutes.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.
