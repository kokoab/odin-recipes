Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a Filipino sour soup that’s both comforting and refreshing, featuring a tamarind-based broth. Typically made with pork, shrimp, or fish, it’s packed with vegetables and delivers a tangy, savory taste that’s perfect for any season.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork ribs or shrimp
  • 1 packet tamarind powder (40g) or 1/2 cup fresh tamarind juice
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 cup green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or spinach
  • 1 small eggplant, sliced
  • 1 long green chili (siling pansigang)
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add pork ribs (if using) and simmer for 45 minutes until tender. If using shrimp, add later.
  2. Add onion, tomatoes, and tamarind powder (or fresh tamarind juice). Simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Add radish and eggplant. Cook for 5-7 minutes until slightly tender.
  4. Stir in green beans and green chili. Cook for 3 minutes.
  5. If using shrimp, add now and cook until pink, about 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add kangkong or spinach and fish sauce. Simmer for 1-2 minutes.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt. Serve hot with rice.
